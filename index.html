Books

My list of must read books that were/are fundamental for my learning... I still have them at the reach of my hand!

I - GAMES and Industry related

Masters of DOOM: How Two Guys Created an Empire and Transformed Pop Culture

A must read book for Gamers. You will discover how John Carmack and John Romero created the technology that changed the gaming industry forever. I am a big fan of the original Quake. I still have it installed in my machine!


Game Engine Black Book: Wolfenstein 3D

Fabien Sanglard did a great job explaining how Idsoftware revolutionized the gaming industry when computers were not built to support games, especially 3D games. If you like history, assembly and games, you will have a great time reading this book.


II - Programming

Programming: Principles and Practice Using C++

If you want to have a solid base on C++, this is your must-read book. The author Bjarne Stroustrup is the creator of C++.


A Tour of C++ 2nd Edition

If you already know how to use another language or want to get a quick update about C++17, this book is for you. Fewer than 250 pages that will give you a lot of information. Another great book from Bjarne Stroustrup


Assembly Language: For x86 Processors

A very well-written book that teaches you Assembly for Intel processors. This book will help you to understand everything related to Assembly.


Computer Organization and Design: Risk-V Edition

This book from D. Patterson and J. Hennessy is extremely interesting for those that wants to know more about computer hardware such as how the CPU and cache works. Also this new version uses the RISK-V instead of MIPS. This book was recommended during my Computer Science degree and I really enjoyed it. Moreover, I'm a big fan of John Carmak (Wolfenstein, DOOM and Quake) and he recommended this book on Twitter.


Graphics Programming Black Book Special Edition

Yes, again, this book from Michael Abrash was also recommended in the same tweet from J. Carmak. If you want to know more about code optimization, game engines, etc this book will be interesting. However, be prepared to make your brain work a lot if you are not familiar with assembly language. Some chapters are very challenging and also talks about the hardware from the 90's.
